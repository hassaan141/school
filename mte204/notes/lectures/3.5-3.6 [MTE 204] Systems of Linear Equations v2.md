3.6 Systems of Linear Equations [Gaussian Elimination-Pivoting+scaling]
• A well-conditioned system is one where a small change in the coefficients leads
to a similar magnitude of change in the results.
• An ill-conditioned system occurs when small changes in the coefficients lead to
large changes in the solution (or a wide range of answers can approximately
satisfy the equations).

3.6 Systems of Linear Equations [Gaussian Elimination-Pivoting+Scaling]
• Partial pivoting
– Avoid divide by zero issues (if any pivot elements are zero)
– Also helps to avoid round-off errors that may be caused by small (non-zero) pivot element
values.
– * note that round-off errors can be cumulative in Gaussian elimination since each result
depends on previous results (error accumulates). This is an important issue for large numbers
of equations.
• Scaling
– Scaling or normalizing the equations – dividing by the largest coefficient, so that the maximum
coefficient is 1.0.

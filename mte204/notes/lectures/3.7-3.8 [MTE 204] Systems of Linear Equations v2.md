3.7 Systems of Linear Equations [Gauss Seidel – Indirect Method]
Direct methods for solving systems of linear equations:
• Gaussian elimination
• Other methods (LU decomposition, matrix inversion)
Indirect (iterative) methods
• Gauss Seidel – employs initial guesses, and then we iterate to converge on a
solution.
• This formulation is well-suited to large numbers of equations and doesn’t induce
the round-off error seen in Gaussian elimination.
– In iterative methods, round-off errors do not accumulate (as they do in Gaussian elimination)
since each iteration is essentially a new approximation to the solution.
– We do have round-off in Gauss-Seidel, but it is only associated with the current iteration
(whereas in Gaussian elimination, error can accumulate for the sequence of steps in the
solution)
• The method has improved efficiency, doesn’t store or make calculations using
zero values.

3.8 Systems of Linear Equations [Gauss Seidel w/ Relaxation]
Gauss Seidel is an indirect (iterative) method for solving systems of linear equations.
• Algorithm is easy to implement.
• Efficient, particularly for banded matrices.
• Can be slow to converge and can diverge.
Two approaches to verify convergence and improve the convergence characteristics
of the Gauss Seidel solution.
1. Convergence criterion (diagonal dominant).
2. Relaxation (changes the convergence rate).

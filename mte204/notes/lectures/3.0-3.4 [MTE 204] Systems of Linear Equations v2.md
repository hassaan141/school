3.4 Systems of Linear Equations [Gaussian Elimination]
• Solving systems of linear equations requires significant computations for more
than 3 x 3 problems.
• Ideal problem for computer-based solution, where we may have thousands or
millions of simple operations (e.g. addition, subtraction, multiplication,
division).
• Gaussian Elimination is a direct method:
e.g. Gaussian Elimination, we solve for the ‘exact’ solution in one pass through the equations.
• Aside, later we will discuss Indirect, iterative methods
e.g. Gauss Seidel, we approximate the solution (initial guesses) and iterate to converge to a final
solution within a tolerance level.

Vectors
Scalars represent physical quantities that have associated with them only a
magnitude (e.g. temperature, density, area, moment of inertia, speed) and can be
represented by real numbers.
Vectors represent physical quantities that have associated with them both
magnitude and direction (e.g. velocity, acceleration, force).
